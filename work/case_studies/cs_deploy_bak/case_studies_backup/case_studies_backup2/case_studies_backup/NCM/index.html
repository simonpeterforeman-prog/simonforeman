01 — The Challenge & Approach

The moment someone says yes is the moment you start losing them.

New cardmembers are most receptive the instant they're approved. But attention fades fast. Without a deliberate plan for the first year, early excitement becomes passive indifference — and eventually churn.

The question: How do you take a brand-new relationship and make it stick?

Three principles that shape everything.

The new cardmember program isn't a campaign — it's a system. Every touchpoint, across every channel, for 13 months, is built on three ideas:

Act early while attention is highest. Coordinate across every channel so nothing competes or contradicts. Measure behavior — not just opens.

04 — Account Setup

From approval to active — all in one session.

Approval flows directly into guided account setup — app download, paperless statements, rewards activation, and wallet provisioning. Customers who complete setup actions in the first session show significantly higher long-term engagement.

06 — Engaged Cardmember Journey
Happy Path

Deeper engagement, not just more messages.

Active cardmembers receive usage-focused comms — rewards, provisioning, features, and education — designed to deepen the relationship. Segmented by purchase activity and engagement level, these journeys surface the right features, partnerships, and cross-sell moments at the right time.

Consistent, personal creative builds brand familiarity through servicing and benefit education. Dynamic content turns milestones — first statement, earnings updates, anniversaries — into moments that feel personal, not promotional.

Partner promotions and cross-sell are timed to established usage patterns — once the card is already part of daily life.

07 — Re-engagement Journey
Unhappy Path

No activity? Escalate before they drift.

Non-activators and early inactives get a dedicated cadence — frequent early, sustained over 12 months — with messaging tailored by segment and tested against barriers to use. Every inaction triggers the next step in a structured re-engagement arc.

Silence is a signal, not a dead end. Escalating outreach with the right mix of urgency and value brings accounts back before drift becomes churn.

Activation Journey

Starting at Day 12, a progressive series of activation emails with increasing urgency — card confirmation, benefit reminders, and direct calls to action.

Incentive Offers & Direct Mail

For persistent non-activators, targeted incentive offers and direct mail with statement credits drive a purchase. Segmented by card-on-file status for maximum relevance.

08 — Challenges & Iteration

The program didn't get better by accident.

It got better because things broke — and every challenge produced a stronger system.

What we ran into
  • Stakeholder turnover reset priorities mid-stream
  • Activation rates trended down across key segments
  • Premium booklet, incentive campaigns, and paid media pilot all failed ROI
  • A full brand refresh required reworking every touchpoint simultaneously
What it produced
  • Product owner interviews to realign on real needs — not inherited wishlists
  • Deep-dive research and workshops that changed how we target and message
  • Killed underperformers, kept the learnings — redirected budget to what worked
  • Stronger creative system built to flex with the brand, not against it
09 — Takeaways

What hundreds of campaigns taught us.

Four principles that emerged from nearly two decades of testing, iterating, and optimizing new customer journeys at scale.

1

Be ready on day one

Customers are most receptive the moment they say yes. The strategy, content, and account plan need to be ready before they arrive.

2

Read the signals

Actions reveal intent. Use them to trigger the right message at the right time, then test to make sure.

3

Don't let them drift

Silence is a signal, not a dead end. Escalating outreach with the right mix of urgency and value brings accounts back.

4

Service earns trust

Higher opens. Higher clicks. Service comms earn trust that marketing can't buy. Use it carefully, and it drives real action.
